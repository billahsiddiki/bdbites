Section 0: Honest Assessment — Read This First

Kill Risk #1: Coordinated Inauthentic Behavior (CIB) Running "pro, anti, neutral" channels secretly under one entity will get you permanently banned. Meta's Q4 2025 transparency report removed over 2,000 CIB networks globally. If your channels share the same IP, device fingerprints, ad accounts, payment methods, or posting patterns — they will get flagged and banned together. All of them. At once.

What to do instead: Run multiple channels with distinct editorial voices (politics-focused, lifestyle-focused, entertainment-focused, Bangla-language, English-language) — all under one transparent parent brand. Different verticals, same parent. That's legal, platform-compliant, and stronger for brand trust.
Kill Risk #2: "Nobody will know it's AI." They will. And when they find out — and they will — your credibility is destroyed overnight.

The smarter play: Don't market it as AI, but don't hide it either. If someone asks, you say "we use AI tools in our workflow like every modern newsroom." The BBC, Reuters, and AP all use AI in their workflows now.
Kill Risk #3: "There's no copyright law in Bangladesh." Bangladesh is a signatory to the Berne Convention and has the Copyright Act 2000 (amended 2005). Enforcement is weak, but platform-level enforcement (Content ID, DMCA) will take your content down regardless of local law.

What to do instead: Aggregate and rewrite. Never copy-paste. Always credit sources. Use free/licensed stock imagery or generate your own visuals with AI.
What Is Genuinely Strong About This Vision
  • AI-first operations in a market where competitors use 20–25 person teams — massive cost advantage.
  • No one in Bangladesh is doing full-pipeline AI automation from scraping to posting.
  • The Bangla bite-size market is still wide open.

Stage 3: GENERATE (Content Creation)

Workflow for a single photo card:

  • AI selects template based on content type (politics = template A, entertainment = template B).
  • AI writes headline (max 8–12 words Bangla, 10–15 words English).
  • AI writes 2–3 line summary.
  • AI selects/generates background image.
  • Bannerbear composites everything into final image.
  • Output: 1080×1080 (Instagram/Facebook), 1080×1920 (Stories/TikTok/Reels).

Tools: Canva Pro ($15/mo) for templates, Bannerbear ($49/mo) for automated generation, FLUX 1.1 Pro (~$0.04/image) for AI illustrations, Ideogram 3 ($8/mo) for text rendering.

Workflow for a 30–60 second news video:

  • AI writes script (3–5 sentences, punchy, conversational).
  • ElevenLabs generates Bangla or English voiceover (Bengali fully supported with voice cloning as of March 2026).
  • AI selects relevant images/footage.
  • CapCut composites: voiceover + images + text overlays + transitions.
  • Auto-add captions (critical for Facebook autoplay).
  • Output: 9:16 vertical video with hook in first 2 seconds.

Tools: ElevenLabs v3 ($22/mo Creator — Bengali supported), CapCut API (Free), OpusClip ($15/mo).

No AI Avatars. HeyGen, Synthesia, and D-ID produce recognizably AI faces. In Bangladesh, this will get called out. Use voiceover + image montage instead — it's the standard format for BD bite-size video and nobody expects a presenter.

Section 6: Automation Architecture (Technical)

System Flow

[NEWS SOURCES] → [SCRAPER (Apify/Custom)] → [DATABASE (Supabase)] ↓ [AI SCORING ENGINE (Claude API)] ↓ [CONTENT QUEUE (scored + prioritized)] ↓ ┌─────────────────┼─────────────────┐ ↓ ↓ ↓ [WRITE (Claude)] [DESIGN (Bannerbear)] [VIDEO (ElevenLabs+CapCut)] ↓ ↓ ↓ └─────────────────┼─────────────────┘ ↓ [CHANNEL ROUTER] (assigns content to correct channel + platform) ↓ [SCHEDULER (Publer + Meta API)] ↓ [PUBLISH across 20 accounts] ↓ [ENGAGEMENT ENGINE] (auto-reply, auto-like, community management) ↓ [ANALYTICS COLLECTOR] ↓ [WEEKLY AI REVIEW] (adjusts scoring weights, templates, posting times) ↓ [LOOPS BACK TO SCORING ENGINE]

n8n Workflows

Workflow 1: Scrape → Score → Queue (Every 10 min)

  • Trigger: Cron (every 10 minutes)
  • HTTP Request: Pull latest from Apify scrapers
  • Code Node: Deduplicate against Supabase
  • HTTP Request: Send to Claude API for scoring
  • IF Node: Score >= 60?
  • Yes → Supabase: Insert into content queue
  • No → Discard

Workflow 2: Queue → Generate → Schedule (Every 15 min)

  • Supabase: Fetch top-scored unprocessed items
  • Claude API: Generate headline + caption + carousel text
  • Bannerbear API: Generate image from template
  • Publer/Meta API: Schedule post
  • Supabase: Mark processed, store post IDs

Workflow 3: Engage (Every 30 min)

  • Fetch new comments from Meta API
  • Claude API: Generate contextual reply draft
  • Phase 1: Store in queue for human review. Phase 2+: Auto-post low-risk replies.
  • Log to Supabase

Workflow 4: Analyze + Improve (Weekly)

  • Trigger: Every Monday 6 AM BST
  • Pull analytics from all platforms
  • Claude API: Pattern analysis, identify top/bottom performers
  • Update scoring weights in Supabase
  • Email weekly performance report

The Neutrality Framework

Every political/controversial post follows this structure:

  • State the fact — what happened, who said what, with source.
  • Provide context — why it matters, historical background.
  • Show both sides — what supporters say, what critics say.
  • Ask the question — let the audience decide.
  • Never editorialize in the post itself.

This makes you impossible to attack. People argue in the comments — which the algorithm loves.

Hook Formulas (Hard-coded into AI prompts)

  • Question Hook: "আপনি কি জানেন বাংলাদেশে প্রতিদিন কত টাকা ঘুষ হয়?"
  • Contrast Hook: "তিনি ১০ বছর আগে রিকশা চালাতেন। আজ তিনি..."
  • Number Hook: "৭টি কারণ যে জন্য ঢাকা বিশ্বের সবচেয়ে..."
  • Revelation Hook: "এই তথ্যটি কেউ বলবে না আপনাকে"
  • Authority Hook: "[Famous person] just said something that changes everything"
  • Controversy Hook: Present both sides → "What do you think?"

18. Crisis Mode Protocol

2. The Five Kill Risks

Kill Risk #1: A Bad AI Comment Reply Goes Viral
One tone-deaf, politically biased AI reply gets screenshotted and shared 50K times = brand finished.

Phase 1 (Months 1–3): NO auto-post replies. AI generates drafts → you review top 20 daily (15 min). Auto-like only.
Phase 2 (Months 3–6): Auto-reply for LOW-RISK only (compliments, factual questions). Political/emotional → NEVER auto-reply.
Phase 3 (Month 6+): Expand gradually. Always keep human gate for political/religious.
Kill Risk #2: Publishing False Information
One wrong fact on Day 5 = nobody trusts you on Day 50.

Solution: Hard speed limits by source tier (detailed in Fact-Checking Layer). Tier 1: 5 min. Tier 4 (social media): NEVER as fact. Image verification via reverse search for EVERY news image.
Kill Risk #3: Platform Account Suspension
Not from CIB — from violating TOS through unofficial automation.

Meta: Business Suite API = legal. Unofficial tools (Jarvee etc.) = instant ban.
TikTok: Use Publer's official integration or TikTok Content Posting API.
YouTube: Remove ALL watermarks. YouTube deprioritizes competing-platform watermarks.
Kill Risk #4: Bangla Writing Sounds Like AI
AI Bangla tells: overly formal register, unnatural word order, missing code-switching, repetitive structure.

Fix: Generate 50 sample captions. Show to 5 BD friends WITHOUT telling them it's AI. If more than 1 in 5 spots it, rewrite prompts. This is a $0 test that prevents a $50,000 mistake.
Kill Risk #5: You Burn Out in Month 2
A fully automated system still needs a human. Minimum: ~45 min/day in Months 1–3, dropping to ~20 min/day by Month 6. Spot-check posts, review flagged comments, check error alerts, approve sponsors. This is not optional.

3. Production Reliability — 3 AM Failures

n8n Error Handling

Dedicated "Error Handler" workflow. CRITICAL (pipeline stopped) = Telegram alert + email. WARNING (single post failed) = log to Google Sheet, review Monday.

Critical triggers: Scraper returns 0 results for >30 min, Claude API errors for >3 calls, Supabase connection fails, Publer posting fails for >5 posts.

Retry logic: Every API node: Retry on Fail = ON, Max Retries = 3, Wait = 5000ms.

7. What "Best of the Best" Actually Means

"Best" does NOT mean most expensive. Sprout Social at $249/mo is not better than Metricool at $22/mo for your stage. Enterprise tools are built for enterprise problems. You have startup problems: speed, cost-efficiency, flexibility.

"Best" DOES mean:

  • Most reliable — fewest outages, best uptime.
  • Best integration — works with n8n, has an API.
  • Scales with you — doesn't force $500/mo jump at a threshold.
  • Output quality — audience can't distinguish from human work.

Claude Sonnet 4.6 IS the best AI for editorial judgment. n8n IS the best orchestration for self-hosted automation. Bannerbear IS the best for template-based image generation at scale. ElevenLabs IS the best for multilingual voice. These aren't compromises. These are the actual best tools for a sub-$700/month AI media operation in 2026.

The day you're spending $5K/month is the day you upgrade to enterprise-tier everything. That day is Year 2, not Day 1.
